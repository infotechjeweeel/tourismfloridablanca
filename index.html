History of Floridablanca

Floridablanca, predating its establishment, comprised haciendas, referring to estates. Specifically, it was a plantation estate aligned with the order of Saint Augustine. Founded in 1823 as a hacienda by Spanish friars, it fell under Lubao Parish's jurisdiction, overseen by a capellan. Initially named Hacienda de San Jose de Calampaui, the site featured a chapel honoring Saint Joseph. Capellanes' records from 1867 indicated the renaming of the hacienda to "Pueblo de Floridablanca." The town's name, interpreted as "MaputingSampaga" in the vernacular and "white flower" in English, sparked debates about its origin, with one theory linking it to a purported visit by Conde de Floridablanca in the early 1800s, a claim unsupported by Philippine history.
